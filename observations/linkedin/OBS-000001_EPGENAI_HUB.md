## Primary claim

The European Parliament is developing a controlled AI platform called EPGenAI Hub to provide approved access to AI models while reducing exposure of sensitive parliamentary information through public services.

## Claim type

- Architectural
- Operational
- Institutional

## Evidence type

- E4 - external reporting
- E8 - institutional reporting, partial

## What appears supported

- The platform is being developed or tested.
- Its purpose is controlled institutional AI access.
- Multiple model providers may be available.
- Security, governance, and data protection are stated objectives.

## What is not yet established

- Actual security effectiveness
- Reduction of hallucinations
- Legislative correctness
- Complete architecture
- Operational deployment
- Long-term governance
- Elimination of shadow AI use
- Full sovereign control of infrastructure

## Hidden assumptions

- Controlled access improves overall governance.
- Institutional deployment guarantees safer use.
- Model plurality improves correctness.
- Centralization reduces risk without increasing correlated failure.
- Approved access closes unofficial access routes.

## Pressure tests

- Secure access != accurate output
- Platform governance != claim governance
- Institution-managed interface != sovereign AI
- AI-assisted drafting != verified legislation
- Audit logging != democratic accountability
- Multiple models != independent evidence
- Approved route exists != shadow route closed
- Centralized control != centralized correctness
