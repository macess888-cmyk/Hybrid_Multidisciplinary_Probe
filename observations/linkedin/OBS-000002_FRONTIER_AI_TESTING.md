## Primary Observation

A public discussion argues that stable, technically independent AI testing capability is becoming a strategic national requirement as frontier AI systems increase in capability.

## What Appears Supported

- Leadership continuity affects institutional capability.
- AI evaluation requires specialized technical expertise.
- Independent testing is presented as distinct from developer self-evaluation.
- Measurement capability is described as a national asset.

## What Is Not Yet Established

- That current capability is insufficient.
- That leadership changes directly reduce testing quality.
- Long-term organizational outcomes.
- Comparative effectiveness of different institutional models.

## Hidden Assumptions

- Stable leadership improves technical independence.
- Government testing remains sufficiently independent.
- Measurement quality scales with institutional continuity.

## Pressure Tests

- leadership stability != technical correctness
- independent testing != complete objectivity
- model evaluation != deployment authorization
- measurement != governance
- evaluation != execution approval
