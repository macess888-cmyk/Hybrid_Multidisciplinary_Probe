# LinkedIn Source Review Workflow

## 1. Capture

Record:

- author
- organization
- publication date
- post or article title
- visible URL
- linked websites
- attached images or documents
- quoted sources
- relevant comments

## 2. Identify source status

Classify as:

- original source
- secondary summary
- repost
- commentary
- promotional material
- public discussion

## 3. Extract claims

Separate:

- technical claims
- architectural claims
- empirical claims
- policy claims
- legal claims
- priority claims
- marketing claims
- predictions
- recommendations

## 4. Classify evidence

Assign the applicable E0-E10 evidence level.

Do not promote social engagement, credentials, or repetition into evidence.

## 5. Inspect linked sources

Where accessible, inspect:

- official websites
- government pages
- repositories
- standards
- papers
- advisories
- cited reports
- organizational connections

## 6. Reconstruct source genealogy

Determine whether multiple posts rely on:

- one primary report
- independent investigations
- copied language
- syndicated material
- shared organizational sources

Multiple links do not automatically represent multiple independent confirmations.

## 7. Pressure test

Record:

- what is supported
- what is not established
- hidden assumptions
- category collapses
- counterexamples
- missing carriers
- potential reproduction paths

## 8. Map relationships

Connect:

person
→ organization
→ project
→ claim
→ evidence
→ standard
→ government body
→ implementation
→ unresolved dependency

## 9. Classify

Use:

PASS
PARTIAL
FAIL
HOLD
NOT TESTED

## 10. Preserve follow-up

Record:

- official-source checks
- technical-verification opportunities
- later corrections
- deployment updates
- independent reproductions
