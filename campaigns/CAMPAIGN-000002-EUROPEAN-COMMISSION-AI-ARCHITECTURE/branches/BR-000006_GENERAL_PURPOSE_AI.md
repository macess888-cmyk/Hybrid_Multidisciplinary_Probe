# BR-000006

Title:

General-Purpose AI

Status:

OPEN

Purpose:

Inspect the legal and institutional architecture governing general-purpose AI models under the EU AI Act.

Primary Focus:

- GPAI provider obligations
- systemic-risk classification
- technical documentation
- copyright-compliance policy
- training-content summaries
- model evaluation
- adversarial testing
- serious-incident reporting
- cybersecurity
- notification to the European Commission
- open-source exemptions
- codes of practice
- implementation guidance

Primary Source:

SRC-000004 - General-purpose AI obligations under the AI Act

Relevant Authorities:

- European Commission
- European AI Office
- national competent authorities

Relevant Legal Instrument:

LEG-000001 - Regulation (EU) 2024/1689

Directly Supported:

- GPAI providers are subject to defined legal obligations.
- Additional obligations apply to GPAI models classified as presenting systemic risk.
- Those additional obligations include evaluation, risk mitigation, incident reporting, and cybersecurity measures.
- The European Commission and European AI Office hold defined implementation and oversight roles.
- Guidance, templates, and voluntary codes support implementation.

Not Established:

- That compliance guarantees model safety.
- That provider documentation is complete or independently reproducible.
- That systemic-risk classification fully captures dangerous capability.
- That reported incidents represent all relevant failures.
- That model evaluation establishes internal motive.
- That observed harmful behavior should be classified as malicious.
- That voluntary codes provide equivalent assurance to binding legal obligations.

Pressure Tests:

- GPAI classification != complete capability classification
- systemic risk != malicious intent
- provider documentation != independent verification
- adversarial testing != exhaustive testing
- incident reporting != complete observability
- cybersecurity obligation != demonstrated cybersecurity
- compliance != correctness
- regulation != causal explanation
- guidance != law
- code of practice != certification
- legal authority != technical capacity

ITR-004 Relevance:

INDIRECT

This branch may support classification of:

- unsafe behavior
- systemic-risk behavior
- cybersecurity failure
- incident-producing behavior
- insufficiently evaluated behavior

It does not by itself support classification of AI behavior as malicious or intentional.

Open Questions:

- How is systemic risk operationally determined?
- Which evaluation methods are mandatory?
- Which results must be independently reproducible?
- What qualifies as a serious incident?
- How are provider claims challenged?
- What evidence supports model designation decisions?
- How are open-source exemptions bounded?
- Which obligations are enforced directly by the AI Office?
- Which obligations remain dependent on provider self-reporting?
- How are model updates and capability changes re-evaluated?
