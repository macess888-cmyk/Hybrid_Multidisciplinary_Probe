# Institutional Position

The European AI Office is established within the European Commission and forms part of the administrative structure of the Directorate-General for Communications Networks, Content and Technology.

---

# Purpose

Support implementation of the EU AI Act, particularly the rules governing general-purpose AI models, while contributing to AI evaluation, compliance, innovation, international cooperation, and coordination with Member States and expert bodies.

---

# Directly Supported

- The AI Office is established within the European Commission.
- It supports implementation and coherent application of the AI Act.
- It supervises and enforces rules applying to general-purpose AI models.
- It develops evaluation tools, methodologies, and benchmarks.
- It may evaluate general-purpose AI models.
- It may request information and corrective measures from providers.
- It contributes to investigations of possible infringements.
- It prepares guidance, guidelines, codes of practice, and implementation tools.
- It works with Member States, the AI Board, the Scientific Panel, the Advisory Forum, and other stakeholders.
- National authorities retain responsibilities for supervising and enforcing rules concerning many AI systems at Member State level.

---

# Authority Boundaries

- The AI Office does not replace all national competent authorities.
- Its establishment does not remove the powers of other EU institutions, agencies, Commission departments, or Member State authorities.
- Its guidance role is distinct from binding legislation.
- Its primary direct enforcement role concerns general-purpose AI models.
- Market-surveillance authorities retain enforcement responsibilities for many AI systems, including high-risk systems and prohibited practices.

---

# Not Established

- That the AI Office has independently validated every evaluation method it uses.
- That assigned legal authority proves operational evaluation capability.
- That model evaluation guarantees deployment safety.
- That compliance guarantees technical correctness.
- That guidance is legally binding in every case.
- That institutional coordination eliminates fragmented or correlated error.
- That observed model behavior demonstrates intent, hostility, or malice.
- That third-party EU model-evaluation capacity is fully operational at present.

---

# Pressure Tests

- assigned authority != demonstrated capability
- evaluation mandate != validated evaluation method
- legal supervision != technical correctness
- guidance != legislation
- compliance != safety
- investigation != proof of causation
- corrective authority != correction effectiveness
- centralized coordination != independent confirmation
- general-purpose AI oversight != complete AI-system oversight
