# Purpose

Summarise the obligations applying to providers of general-purpose AI models and the additional obligations applying to providers of general-purpose AI models with systemic risk.

---

# Directly Supported

- Providers of general-purpose AI models must prepare technical documentation.
- Providers must implement a copyright-compliance policy.
- Providers must publish a summary of model training content.
- Providers of general-purpose AI models with systemic risk face additional obligations.
- Those additional obligations include notification to the Commission.
- They also include risk assessment and mitigation.
- They include serious-incident reporting.
- They include cybersecurity protections.
- General-purpose AI obligations entered into application on 2 August 2025.
- The GPAI Code of Practice is a voluntary compliance tool.
- Commission guidelines clarify the scope of GPAI-provider obligations.
- A mandatory template supports public summaries of training content.

---

# Authority Boundaries

- The source summarises obligations but does not replace the AI Act.
- Commission guidelines are interpretive and are not themselves binding legislation.
- The Code of Practice is voluntary.
- Provider compliance does not independently establish model safety.
- Regulatory classification does not establish internal model intent.
- Notification does not prove the presence or absence of systemic risk.
- Documentation does not prove technical correctness.

---

# Not Established

- That technical documentation is complete or accurate in every case.
- That reported risk assessments are independently reproducible.
- That incident reporting captures all serious incidents.
- That cybersecurity protections are effective.
- That compliance eliminates systemic risk.
- That model evaluation establishes autonomous motive or malice.
- That compute thresholds alone reliably classify every dangerous model.
- That provider self-reporting is sufficient for independent verification.

---

# Pressure Tests

- documentation != correctness
- notification != independent verification
- risk classification != demonstrated harm
- systemic risk != malicious intent
- incident reporting != complete observability
- code adherence != technical safety
- legal compliance != operational reliability
- compute threshold != complete capability assessment
- provider declaration != independent evidence
- model evaluation != causal explanation

# ITR-004 Relevance

INDIRECT

The source supports distinctions among systemic risk, unsafe behavior, incident reporting, cybersecurity failure, and regulatory supervision.
