## Purpose

The Hybrid Multidisciplinary Probe exists to independently observe, inspect, compare, and pressure test technical, scientific, institutional, governmental, and architectural claims while preserving evidence, uncertainty, and traceability.

The objective is not to prove ideas correct or incorrect.

The objective is to determine what conclusions are supported by the available evidence within a clearly defined review boundary.

---

## Scope

The observatory may inspect:

- technical papers
- government publications
- standards
- GitHub repositories
- security advisories
- LinkedIn articles
- company white papers
- AI systems
- governance frameworks
- quantum research
- cybersecurity research
- public technical discussions

---

## Primary Principle

No claim is accepted because of:

- authority
- popularity
- reputation
- publication venue
- number of citations
- organizational prestige

Every claim is evaluated according to evidence.

## Review Philosophy

Observation precedes explanation.

Evidence precedes conclusion.

Unknowns are preserved.

Classification remains proportional to evidence.

---

## Evidence First

Claims never inherit evidence.

Evidence never inherits authority.

Authority never inherits correctness.

---

## Classification

PASS

PARTIAL

FAIL

NOT TESTED

---

## Review Boundary

The observatory evaluates:

what is claimed;

what evidence exists;

what remains unknown;

what is outside scope.

The observatory does not create evidence where none exists.

---

## Independence

The observatory remains independent of:

authors;

organizations;

products;

vendors;

institutions;

government;

review outcomes.

The same methodology applies to all.
